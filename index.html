Top Albums

OK Computer (1997)

A groundbreaking album that blended alternative rock with experimental elements. It explores themes of technology, alienation, and societal anxiety.

Kid A (2000)

A bold departure from their earlier sound, Kid A embraced electronic music and abstract lyrics, creating a haunting and atmospheric experience.

The Bends (1995)

Known for its melodic rock and heartfelt lyrics, this album cemented Radiohead as a force in the Britpop era while hinting at their experimental future.

In Rainbows (2007)

Released using a pay-what-you-want model, this album features a lush and intimate sound, combining emotional depth and melodies.

Amnesiac (2001)

Recorded during the same sessions as Kid A, Amnesiac delves deeper into experimental and jazz influences, with a darker and more fragmented tone.

A Moon Shaped Pool (2016)

A somber and orchestral work, this album is rich with layered arrangements and reflects themes of loss, memory, and environmental concerns.
